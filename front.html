Cigarette smoking is detrimental to mental health, increasing the risk of anxiety, depression, and other mental illnesses. Nicotine, the addictive substance in tobacco, can initially alleviate stress and anxiety, but long-term use can worsen symptoms of these conditions.

Furthermore, smoking has been linked to a higher risk of suicidal ideation and suicide attempts, particularly among individuals with pre-existing mental health conditions.

A 2019 study of nearly 30,000 e-cigarette users found that current users had double the odds of having a diagnosis of depression compared to those who had never vaped.

A more recent story found that tobacco-related diseases accounted for approximately 53% of deaths among people with schizophrenia, 48% among htose with bipolar disorder, and 50% among those with depression.
